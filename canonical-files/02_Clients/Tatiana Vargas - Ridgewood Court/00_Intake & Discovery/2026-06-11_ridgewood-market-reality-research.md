# RIDGEWOOD COURT — MARKET REALITY RESEARCH
*Evie's research, Jun 11 2026. Internal — informs strategy, NOT shown to client.*

## Address / location
- **~6360–6388 Ridgewood Court Dr, Jackson, MS 39211** (Hinds County), on **E County Line Road**, <1 mile east of the I-55 interchange (~33,000 VPD).
- Sits **right on the Hinds/Madison county line** — County Line Rd is literally the border between **North Jackson (Hinds)** and affluent **Ridgeland/Madison**. NorthPark Mall is just across, on the Ridgeland side.

## The real market dynamic (why the OM looks rosier than the curb)
- The OM's strong 5-mile numbers (AHHI ~$89K, net worth ~$782K, $1B spending power) are **pulled UP by Ridgeland & Madison County**, which are wealthy and growing. The immediate Jackson side is lower-income and higher-crime.
- **Jackson proper:** crime ~131% above national avg (violent +79%, property +142%). Documented affluent-retail flight to Madison County (Renaissance at Colony Park); the old Jackson Mall in N. Jackson declined into a medical mall.
- Net: Ridgewood Court is on the **good edge of Jackson, next to money** — it can capture Ridgeland/Madison spillover for errands + family outings, but it currently reads as a Jackson-side value center, and the affluent crowd already has NorthPark + Renaissance.

## What's actually there now (ACTUAL rent roll — from the Site Plan + OM, not web listings)
- **Anchors:** Roses (50,866 SF), Urban Air Adventure Park (34,969), Ross Dress for Less (25,969)
- **Shops:** Rainbow (~12,549), Dollar Tree (10,000), The Mighty Crab (5,958, seafood restaurant), FedEx Office (4,000), Peach Cobbler Cafe (1,500), Verizon (1,280), Smoothie King (1,280), Nail Magic (896)
- → **Discount/off-price + family entertainment + a few small food/service shops.** That's the proven demand.
- **Vacant ≈ 91,292 SF (~38%):** four big boxes (~25,020 / 25,000 / 24,031 / 10,500 SF) + small shop spaces (1,700 / 2,301 / 1,460 / 1,280). The big boxes are the whole job.
- **At Home** is physically on the site but is its **own parcel (NAP — Not A Part)** — a co-located national draw, NOT part of Ridgewood Court's leasable space. Good for positioning (national anchor traffic next door), but not a vacancy/tenant Tatiana manages.
- ⚠️ **NOT in the center** (despite some web listings): TJ Maxx, Old Navy, pOpshelf — do not reference these as tenants.
- **Co-tenancy:** AJ says **Ross** needs another national tenant or can exit; the OM also shows **Rose's** has a co-tenancy clause (reduced-rent period). Keeping/landing national anchors is doubly critical.

## Realistic read on the proposed concepts
**Likely to last (fits the trade area + the co-tenancy need):**
- **A national value/off-price anchor** — TOP priority (Ross co-tenancy clause). Think Burlington, Five Below, Marshalls, Hobby Lobby, Ollie's, Boot Barn — nationals that thrive next to Ross/TJ Maxx.
- **More family entertainment / experiential** anchored off Urban Air's proven draw (arcade-eatery, family fun).
- **Everyday services & value fitness** — urgent care, dental, a big-box gym (Planet Fitness-type, not boutique), nails/hair.
- **Fast-casual / QSR chains.**

**Risky / may not last here (aspirational, mismatched to the immediate market + safety perception):**
- Boutique yoga / ClassPass studios — that crowd is in Ridgeland/Madison and has options; unlikely to cross to the Jackson side.
- Roller rink, speakeasy barber, destination brewery-with-big-lawn — capital-heavy, need an affluent destination crowd + a safety reputation the area doesn't have yet.
- Indie coffee shop — indie coffee lives in walkable affluent districts (Fondren), not a County Line power center.

## Strategic takeaway (for the marketing + lease-up plan)
Lead with what the trade area proves it wants: **value/off-price NATIONAL tenants + family-friendly experiential**, positioned as *"convenient, family-friendly value + everyday experiences"* that pulls both the Jackson value shopper and Ridgeland/Madison families for errands & outings. **Make securing a national tenant the #1 lease-up objective** (protects Ross). Don't over-rotate to boutique/upscale concepts the immediate market won't sustain.
