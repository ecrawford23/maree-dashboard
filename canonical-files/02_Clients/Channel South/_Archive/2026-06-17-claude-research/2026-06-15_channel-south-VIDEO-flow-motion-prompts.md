## THE ONE RULE (paste into every Flow prompt)
Veo will happily melt your building if you let it. Frame every prompt as *life inside a locked scene*:

> **LEAD with life:** *"Animate this scene to life with subtle, realistic motion:"* [then the scene-specific list]
> **END with the lock:** *"Slow, gentle camera move only. Buildings, architecture, and signage stay completely solid and static — no morphing, melting, or shifting structures. Realistic physics and scale. Photorealistic, cinematic, natural light. No warped faces or bodies, no extra text."*

**What animates beautifully in Veo:** water, reflections, clouds, foliage/palms in breeze, fabric, string lights, distant traffic, glowing windows.
**What's risky:** close-up faces (keep people mid-to-far distance), fast or long camera moves (keep them short and slow), anything asking a building to move.
**Always generate 2–3 takes** and keep the one where the architecture holds.
