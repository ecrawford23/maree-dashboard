## The structure — a day arc (scales 90 sec to ~3 min)
Follow the sun. It's the most natural "bring it to life" spine and shows every part of the district.
1. **Heritage cold-open** (sepia, 1903) → 2. **Dawn** (the waterfront wakes) → 3. **Morning** (residents, coffee, amenities) → 4. **Midday** (retail + street life) → 5. **Golden hour** (the hero district light) → 6. **Sunset/dusk** (hotel + rooftop) → 7. **Night/blue hour** (lit-up, alive) → 8. **Brand close.**
Weave **detail/texture b-roll** (Section C) between scenes — that connective tissue is what makes it feel like a film, not a slideshow.

## Two honesty flags before you generate
- **Design-accurate scenes** = built from your real renders. Safe for investors.
- **Vibe b-roll** = invented interiors / ambient shots Veo dreams up (a guest room, a restaurant, waves, coffee). Beautiful and useful, but **don't present invented interiors as the actual built spaces** to capital partners — label them "lifestyle," or get real interior renders from the design team first. Your interiors on hand (lobby, fitness, mail) are **clay/gray models** — usable but Gemini will partly invent materials.
- Every Veo prompt below assumes the **bring-to-life rule**: lead with motion, then *"buildings/architecture stay solid — no morphing; slow camera; photorealistic."*
