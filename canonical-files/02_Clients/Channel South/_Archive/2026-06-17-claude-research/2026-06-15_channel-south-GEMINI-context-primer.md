## THE PROJECT
**Channel South** (a.k.a. City Center Gulfport) — a $105M mixed-use waterfront district under construction in **downtown Gulfport, Mississippi**, on the historic site of the 1903 Great Southern Hotel. It includes:
- **136 residences** (an 80-unit building, a 36-unit mixed-use building, and 20 studio/junior units)
- **Hotel Holden** — a 114-room Marriott Tribute hotel with a rooftop bar
- **Ground-floor retail and restaurants**, a pool/clubhouse, and a 288-space parking deck
- Developed by **Bellamare + AnderCorp**.
These images are for investor and marketing use, so realism and architectural accuracy both matter.

## THE SITE & ORIENTATION (use this for accurate light + views)
- The block sits between **Beach Boulevard (US-90), the waterfront road, on the SOUTH** and **13th Street on the NORTH**, with **27th Avenue (west)** and **25th Avenue (east)**.
- **North is up; the water is to the SOUTH.** Directly south across Beach Blvd are **Jones Park, the Gulfport Municipal Marina, a lighthouse, and the Mississippi Sound.**
- **Hotel Holden** fronts Beach Blvd; its **rooftop bar looks SOUTH** over Jones Park and the marina.
- **The sun sets in the WEST** (the 27th Ave side). At golden hour, **light rakes from the west and shadows point east.** Use this for every exterior — it's the real-world light.

## THE LOOK (the brand)
Editorial coastal restraint — think Alys Beach / Rosemary Beach / 30A: calm, warm, sunlit, unhurried, premium. Late golden hour. One consistent warm color grade across every image. Architecture and light do the talking — no clutter, no neon, no over-saturation.

## THE NON-NEGOTIABLE RULES (every image)
1. **Transform, don't "enhance."** Recreate the scene as a real photograph — never just restyle the render.
2. **Lock the architecture.** Keep buildings, proportions, rooflines, balconies, window layout, storefronts, and signage exactly as shown. Do not redesign, move, add, or remove any structure or text.
3. **Real people, not 3D figures.** Replace every entourage figure with a real photographed person — varied ages and body types, candid mid-stride poses, individual wrinkled clothing, real skin and faces. Keep people mid-to-far distance.
4. **Real cars, not models.** Authentic paint, accurate reflections, faint road grime.
5. **Real, imperfect materials.** Stucco with mottling, brick with mortar/stain variation, glass with true reflections and minor smudges, wet asphalt with realistic reflections, pavers with wear, palms with individual irregular fronds.
6. **Real camera.** Shot on a Sony A7R V, 35mm f/4, ISO 100. Shallow depth of field (far background slightly soft), fine natural film grain, faint lens vignette, subtle atmospheric haze, true-to-life color.
7. **Avoid the CGI tells:** plastic/repeating plants, waxy or cloned people, showroom cars, flat even lighting, everything in focus, over-saturation, spotless surfaces, smooth gradient skies, HDR halos.
