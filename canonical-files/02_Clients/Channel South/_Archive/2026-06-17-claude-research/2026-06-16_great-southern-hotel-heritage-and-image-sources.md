# Great Southern Hotel — Heritage Brief + Archival Images
*Evie · 2026-06-16 · For the Channel South video's heritage open. Facts verified against Preservation in Mississippi + Library of Congress + MDAH postcard collections.*

## The story (verified)
- **Joseph T. Jones** — Civil War veteran and entrepreneur — swept into the coast in the late 1890s buying pinelands, then **founded the city of Gulfport**: he built the First National Bank, the Gulfport Real Estate Improvement Company, Union Station, and ran the **Gulf & Ship Island Railroad**. The Great Southern was his crowning centerpiece — a pleasure palace to draw vacationing Chicagoans and New Yorkers south.
- **Opened June 1903.** A vast **wooden frame structure**, painted **dark olive-green with gray shutters, white trim, and a red clay-tile roof**, set in palm-studded gardens.
- Sat at the **head of 25th Avenue**, facing the **Mississippi Sound and Cat Island**, with **600+ feet of frontage.**
- **Three stories**, sprawling wings, **250 rooms**, one bath per two rooms, a **telephone in every room**, hot & cold running water, and its **own powerhouse**.
- Lobby in antique oak ("Meeting Place of the Entire Middle South"); grand dining room in Flemish oak; café, billiards room, palm room, and a "box ball"/basketball court. Marketed as the **"Aristocrat of Southern Taverns."**
- Built a **pier with a tram out to the Gulfport Yacht Club** at its end (clubhouse burned 1905, rebuilt). In **1908 engaged Donald Ross to design a 9-hole golf course.** Even **Al Capone** was said to turn up in the lobby.
- Closed briefly in the Depression (1933–34), was rescued by local owners, then slowly lost out to modern travel. **Demolished in 1951 for U.S. Highway 90.**
- **Jones Park occupies the original hotel site today.**

> **THE HOOK (and it's literally true):** the Great Southern stood at the **head of 25th Avenue facing the Sound** — directly across Beach Boulevard from where **Hotel Holden now rises at the 25th Avenue corner**. "Same ground. New chapter." is documented fact, not a slogan.

> **Accuracy guardrails for capital partners:** (1) It's the **historic site / same ground**, not the exact footprint — the hotel itself stood where Jones Park + US-90 are now. Say "rises on the same waterfront / at the head of 25th Avenue across from the original site." (2) **Drop "oldest golf course in Mississippi"** — verified detail is only "a Donald Ross 9-hole course, 1908." (3) Structure was **wood frame, olive-green** — not brick.
