## PART 1 · SEO AUDIT + SETUP

### Where it stands today
- **channelsouth.com is password-gated and pre-launch**, so it has **zero organic search presence** right now (nothing relevant is indexed for "Channel South Gulfport" / "downtown Gulfport apartments"). That's normal for pre-open — but it means the SEO job is to **set it up right *before* the gate comes down**, not fix it after.
- **No Google Business Profile yet** (expected — GBP needs a servable location; set this up as the hotel/residences near open). Flag for later.
- **Social profiles aren't optimized for search** — IG/FB search indexes the **Name field + bio**, and right now the handle isn't doing keyword work.

### The meta formula to use (2026 standard)
**Title:** under ~60 characters · **Description:** under ~160 characters · pattern = **Primary Keyword + Location | Value Prop | Brand**.

### Technical / on-page checklist (for the build)
- **LocalBusiness schema** on the homepage + contact page: name, address, phone, hours, geo coordinates, URL, image — and make it **match the Google Business Profile exactly** (Google cross-checks them).
- One `<h1>` per page using the primary keyword + location.
- Image alt text with descriptive, location-rich phrasing (renders are your main images).
- A **/story** page — the Great Southern Hotel history is unique, link-worthy content that earns backlinks and E-E-A-T.
- Set up **Google Business Profile** near open; fill the Products/Services section (it feeds Google AI Overviews).
- Pursue **local backlinks**: Coastal Mississippi, Gulfport Main Street, WLOX, Sun Herald, Mississippi Business Journal (the press coverage already exists — make sure those articles link the site).

### Social-profile SEO (do this now — free, immediate)

- **Bio:** keep the on-voice line but work in searchable terms — *downtown Gulfport, waterfront, Hotel Holden, residences*. (Current bio already does most of this — strong.)

## PART 2 · WHAT'S WORKING ON SOCIAL RIGHT NOW (2026)

### Video is the engine
- **Short-form vertical Reels (up to 90s) outperform static** for real estate + hospitality. The top luxury players (Sotheby's, Douglas Elliman) win with **straightforward walkthroughs — not dances or trends** — just a camera moving through the space.
- **75% of hotel bookings are mobile-first**, increasingly TikTok-driven — so vertical video built for discovery is where new audiences come from. (Matches your data: 57.5% of your views are already non-followers.)

### Authentic > polished
- **Raw, un-edited, human behind-the-scenes** content builds emotional connection and outperforms staged shoots. POV clips, staff/team stories, real moments.
- **UGC + real recommendations** beat glossy influencer content — people trust genuine over staged.
- Trending meta-format: stitch the **polished result** with a "you're so creative!" overlay, then cut to the **chaotic reality** behind it. (Perfect for showing how a render or the video actually gets made — doubles as Marée content.)

### The hospitality mood trends
- **"Calm-cation" / "hushpitality"** — quiet, restorative content that sells the feeling of switching off. Tailor-made for **Hotel Holden's rooftop / sunset / water** content.

### Format mechanics
- **Carousels** for depth and saves: room types, "Stay & Play" multi-day packages, **amenity sequences** (Spa → Pool → Dining). Your *Then & Now* and *Meet the District* ideas are exactly this.
- **Mix Reels + carousels + Stories + Lives** weekly — variety signals relevance to the algorithm.
- **Turn one idea into three formats** — a Reel, a Story sequence, a static post. Triples output from one shoot/render.
- **Trending audio** moves reach — check the current week's trending sounds before posting Reels (tools track these weekly).

## PART 3 · CONTENT-IDEAS BANK FOR CHANNEL SOUTH
*Built from the trends above + your measured top performers (brand graphics = reach, renders = engagement, real moments = both). Adapted for a pre-open development — so "guest" trends become "construction / design / heritage" BTS.*

### Reels (the growth engine — turn the AI video + renders into these)
1. **Render fly-through** of the district set to trending audio — your renders already drive engagement; motion + sound drives reach.
2. **"Watch it rise"** construction time-lapse / progress series — the genre's most reliable performer.
3. **Design reveal** — pan across materials, finishes, the Flick Mars interiors as they come.
4. **Drone/aerial** over the site, Jones Park, and the Harbor — sense of place.
5. **"Day in the future life"** — POV of a morning at Channel South (coffee → waterfront walk → rooftop), concept-narrated over renders.
6. **"You're so creative / the reality"** — the polished render vs. the messy process of making it (Marée flex + authentic trend).

### Carousels (depth + saves)
7. **Then & Now** — Great Southern Hotel (1903) ↔ Channel South (2027). Your heritage franchise.
8. **"Meet the District"** — swipe through the amenity sequence: Residences → Retail → Dining → Hotel Holden → Rooftop.
9. **"5 things you didn't know about this corner of Gulfport"** — heritage fun facts (telephones in 1903, Donald Ross golf course, the $1,000 to prove the port).
10. **Residence / floor-plan types** once available — the real-estate carousel that converts.

### Stories (interactive — proven format in this niche)
11. **"Guess the year"** poll on heritage facts → reveal.
12. **This-or-That** on design choices ("rooftop sunset or harbor morning?").
13. **Question box:** "What do you want on the Gulfport waterfront?" — feeds community + ideas.
14. **Countdown stickers** to milestones (parking 2026, hotel 2027).
15. **"Tap to reveal"** Hotel Holden teasers.

### Community / UGC (warmth + reach)
16. **"Share your memory of this corner"** — invite locals' old Great Southern Hotel / downtown photos and reshare. Heritage + UGC + community in one — and it rides the "real over staged" trend.
17. **Reshare + tag** the civic/press wins (WLOX segment, mayor's support) — earned credibility you're not cashing in yet.

### "Hushpitality" mood (for Hotel Holden)
18. **Quiet rooftop / sunset / water** content selling the *feeling* of the place — the restorative trend, perfect for the hotel's "rooted in story, atmosphere, experience" positioning.

> **Cadence trick:** every shoot or render = one Reel + one Story sequence + one static/carousel. One asset, three slots.
