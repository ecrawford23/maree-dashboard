The data says heritage and trendy aren't opposites — they're the same play in 2026:
- **Nostalgia content earns ~+26% engagement** on average (some categories +38–47%), and produces a **2.4× higher emotional response** (dopamine up, cortisol down). People are craving it as a reset against AI-generated sameness.
- **"Place-based storytelling" + local heritage is a top 2026 movement** on TikTok/IG — communities reward authentic local history and pride.
- **Human-led, serialized, recurring storytelling beats polish** — follower count matters less than story quality and fit.

## LIVE TREND SNAPSHOT (June 2026 — these rotate weekly, re-check before posting)
**Formats worth adapting:**
- **"Remember who you are" / transition montage** (selfie → defining moments) → adapt to **the place** remembering itself: archival Gulfport → Channel South rising. Perfect for **Then & Now.**
- **"I Am Home" (secret favorite place is home)** → "the waterfront comes home" — your **"Where Gulfport began, Gulfport returns"** spine. Emotional, on-brand.
- **"Top 5" list Reels** → **"5 things you didn't know about this corner of Gulfport."** Listicles + heritage = high performers.
- **Brand-friendly listing Reels** → **"Meet the District"** amenity walk-through.
- **Archival → render transition** (a perennial, supercharged by the nostalgia wave) → Great Southern 1903 → Hotel Holden.

**Skip (off-brand for a developer):** two-person walk lip-sync, "I have therapy" POV, deadpan meme formats.

**Audio:** trending sounds change weekly (check Buffer / Later / SocialBee trend lists the day you cut a Reel). Pick **instrumental or cinematic** trending audio over lyric-driven meme songs to stay editorial.
