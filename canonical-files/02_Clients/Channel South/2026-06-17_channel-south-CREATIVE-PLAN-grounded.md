## 2 · THE LOOK (locked brand rules)
- **"Channel South" = the buoy logo, always.** Never typed.
- **La Luxes Serif** = serif lines (place/labels). **Brittany** (script) = expressive headlines. Caps support sparingly.
- **Warm, filmic, faded grade** on every image — one cohesive tone across the grid.
- **Palette:** teal/navy · dusty blue · stone/sand · cream · brass.
- Editorial restraint: lots of breathing room, never cluttered, never an "ad."

## 3 · THE POST-TREATMENT SYSTEM (this is what gives it character)
Seven distinct treatments. **Rule: never run the same treatment twice in a row** — that alternation is the whole point.

**A · Full-bleed hero — NO text.** Just the gorgeous render/photo, maybe the small logo. Lets the work breathe. (Heros, rooftop, aerials.)
**B · Whisper caption.** Full photo + one tiny line (Brittany or La Luxes) low-corner + small logo. Quiet, editorial. (Lifestyle, place.)
**C · Heritage editorial.** Archival photo, treated *differently* from the renders — vertical "EST. 1903" running up the side, sepia-leaning, a serif caption. Its own visual world so the history series is recognizable. (Then & Now, Captain Jones.)
**D · Statement / script.** One evocative Brittany line big over a detail crop or a tinted panel — a "moment," not an announcement. ("Channeling the past.")
**E · Carousel story.** Cover (logo + script) → interior frames (one idea each, lots of white space) → CTA frame. (Meet the District, the fun-fact decks.)
**F · Fact / "did you know."** Playful, different energy — a number or fact as the hero (La Luxes), archival texture. (1903 luxuries, "293 million feet.")
**G · Reel cover.** Motion-first; cover frame minimal so the video carries it. (Watch-it-rise, render fly-through, rooftop.)
