*Now we have both platforms.*

**Performance:** Views **5.4K** (+936%) · Viewers **2,190** · **76.1% of views from non-followers** · Interactions **103** (54 follower / 49 non-follower) · FB visits **460** · **Net follows +46, zero unfollows** (+187%). **Top formats (Apr 20–May 17):** Multi-photo/carousel **2,523 views / 43 interactions** (#1 both) · Photo 1,301 / 35 · Reels 975 / 12 · Links 621 / 11 · Stories 2.

**Messaging / leads:** Conversations **0**, new contacts **0**, leads **0**, orders **0** — the Page isn't capturing any inbound, even though a leasing inquiry already came in by phone. **Lead capture is an untapped opportunity** (turn on messaging, add a "DM for leasing/info" CTA).

**The cross-platform verdict (this is the big one):** **Multi-photo / carousel wins on BOTH Instagram and Facebook** — #1 for views *and* interactions on FB, and the save-king on IG. Single photos are a solid #2. Links barely engage (so the "link in bio / link posts" aren't the play — storytelling content is).

**So the format strategy tightens to:**
- **Go carousel-heavy.** Carousels/multi-photo are the single best-performing format on both platforms — lead with them (Then & Now, Meet the District, fun-fact decks, design reveals).
- **Renders/brand graphics as the visuals** inside those carousels (your IG top performers).
- **Reels for reach/discovery** (the growth lever), but they're a supporting act to carousels for engagement.
- **Skip link-only posts** — they're the weakest format; put the link in the bio, not the feed.

**Cross-channel read (the strategic one):**
- **Facebook is your stronger GROWTH channel right now.** Higher non-follower reach (**76%** vs IG's 57.5%) and it's actually *converting* — **+46 net follows, 0 unfollows** in 4 weeks. Don't treat FB as the afterthought.
- **Instagram pulls more raw views but isn't converting visitors** (783 visits → ~0 net follows). The IG-specific fix is the **bio + follow CTA** — the content is fine, the profile isn't closing.
- **Reveals drive the spikes on both** (Hotel Holden = the biggest day). Keep a steady reveal cadence.
- **Capture the demand:** turn on FB/IG messaging + a "DM for leasing/info" CTA — inbound is currently zero despite real leasing interest.

## 3 · WHAT ACTUALLY PERFORMS (format + niche data, 2025–26)
Measured benchmarks from current social data — the realistic targets for an account like this:
- **Carousels are the engagement king:** ~**10%** avg engagement vs ~**6%** for regular Reels and ~**7%** for single image. Carousels win on **saves** (people save what they want to come back to — perfect for "the vision"). 
- **Reels are for reach/new followers; carousels are for depth/warm audience.** Run both in the same week.
- **Real-estate specific:** *national* real-estate accounts struggle; **local, place-specific accounts thrive.** Top-performing moves in this niche: **property/site-tour Reels, "guess the price/year" interactive posts, neighborhood-guide carousels.**
- **Pre-open developments win on storytelling, not listings** — sell the *destination and lifestyle*, treat the whole district as one brand, and collect UGC/asset material early. (National Harbor's geo-targeted IG + storytelling approach had components selling out ahead of schedule.)

**Translation to Channel South:**
1. Make the **history a carousel franchise** (Great Southern Hotel → Channel South) — exactly the save-bait carousels reward.
2. Turn the **AI video / renders into Reels** — that's the reach engine to grow past 116.
3. Use **"guess the year" / "guess the price"** interactive Stories — proven format, and you already have the heritage facts for it.
4. Post **every construction milestone** — progress content is the genre's reliable performer.
