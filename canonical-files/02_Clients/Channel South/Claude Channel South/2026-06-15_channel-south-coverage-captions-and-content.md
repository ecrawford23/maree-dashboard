## GULFPORT / CHANNEL SOUTH / HOTEL HOLDEN — FACT BANK (verified, with sources)
*Pull from here for any caption, Story, or the AI video script. All checked 2026-06-15.*

**Channel South / Hotel Holden**
- $105M, 4.34 acres, 25th Ave & West Beach Blvd, downtown Gulfport. AnderCorp + Bellamare.
- On the Great Southern Hotel site. Phase 1: 136 residences, ~17,637 sq ft retail/dining, 114-room Hotel Holden (Marriott Tribute), 294-space garage.
- Construction began Dec 2025; parking 2026; residences + hotel through 2027.
- Hotel Holden: rooftop over Jones Park + the Harbor; "coastal legacy"; "rooted in story, atmosphere, and experience."

**Great Southern Hotel (1903)**
- Built by Joseph T. Jones, designed by Thomas Sully. 3-story wood, 250 rooms.
- Telephones in each room, hot/cold running water, electric chandeliers, own artesian well, sewage system, power plant. Demolished 1951.

**Great Southern Golf Club (1908)**
- Donald Ross-designed 9-hole course overlooking the Mississippi Sound. Oldest golf course in Mississippi. Closed May 2022 after 114 years.

**Captain Joseph T. Jones (1842–1916, from Pennsylvania)**
- Oil baron — hit oil on his 13th well (1867); became the largest US crude producer.
- Co-founded Gulfport with William H. Hardy; finished the Gulf & Ship Island Railroad.
- Paid to dredge the deep-water channel that created the Port of Gulfport; proved it by paying Capt. Philippo Avegno $1,000 to sail in, Jan 24, 1902.
- "The city's greatest benefactor" — bronze statue dedicated 1942.

**Jones Park (1935)**
- Grace Jones Stewart donated land adjacent to the Great Southern Hotel + port for a public park and small-craft harbor. The view from Hotel Holden's rooftop.

**Maritime naming heritage (from Ian Hanson Studio naming options, Mar 2026 — internal, not public)**
- Themes drawn from: shipwrights, ship chandlers, the carrick bend knot, the Sandhill crane, longleaf pines, the oleander (grew in the Great Southern's gardens). *Outlet names not finalized — keep internal.*
