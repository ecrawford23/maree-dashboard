## THE THROUGH-LINE OF WHAT YOU LOVE
Wharf OB, Rosemary, Seaside, Alys — they look different, but they share one DNA: **coastal place-brands that sell a *feeling* through curated, editorial restraint.** No clutter, no neon, no chain-store energy. The architecture and the light do the talking. That's the exact same instinct as the **Marée voice** (specific, editorial, white space, no hype) — which means the aesthetic you're drawn to *is already on-brand for Channel South.* Good news: you're not fighting your taste, you're following it.

Here's what each one actually contributes:

### The Wharf Orange Beach — *energy + destination* (your favorite)
- **Vibe:** vibrant, breezy, "life happens here." A place you *go* — marina, amphitheater, the ferris wheel, the SPECTRA laser show, waterfront dining.
- **What to steal:** the **destination-as-lifestyle** framing (sell the day, not the building), a signature **"Instagrammable moment"** (their ferris wheel = an icon people photograph), and a **multi-account structure** (@thewharfob + marina + amp + store sub-accounts) — relevant to the open question of whether Hotel Holden / the Flats get their own handles later.
- **Energy level:** the most active/fun of the four — good model for Channel South's **activation + events** layer once open.

### Rosemary Beach — *sophistication + curation*
- **Vibe:** darker, European / West Indies / St. Augustine influence. Dark trim, steep roofs, balconies. **Understated luxury, effortless elegance.**
- **What to steal:** **curated, independently-owned, no-chains/no-neon/no-clutter** positioning (this *is* the Marée voice), and the "timeless traveler" tone. This is the **sophistication ceiling** for Channel South's captions and retail story.

### Seaside — *warmth + nostalgia*
- **Vibe:** lighter, pastel coastal cottage, charm, **coastal nostalgia,** warm and whimsical.
- **What to steal:** the **nostalgia angle** — which Channel South owns for free through the **Great Southern Hotel (1903) heritage.** Seaside leans on coastal memory; you have a *literal* historic landmark on the site.

### Alys Beach — *restraint + the photogenic*
- **Vibe:** stark **white** stucco (Bermuda / Antigua / North African), calm, sunlit, tranquil. Architecture **as** the brand. New Urbanist, walkable, *intensely* photogenic.
- **What to steal:** **restraint as a strategy** — one strong palette, lots of light, let the architecture breathe. Alys proves a clear, disciplined look beats a busy one. This is the model for Channel South's **grid consistency.**

## CHANNEL SOUTH'S OWN AESTHETIC (the recipe)
Pull the four together and filter through Gulfport + Hotel Holden:

**Palette** — Channel South already leans here: deep coastal **teal/slate blue** (the brand card), **Hotel Holden's soft dusty blue**, warm **cream / sand**, and a **brass/maritime navy** accent. Keep it tight — Alys-level discipline. Every render, photo, and graphic color-graded to the **same warm, sunlit, calm** tone.

**Type** — the elegant **serif + script** of the existing Channel South logo ("Gulfport, Mississippi") is exactly the Rosemary/30A sophistication register. Protect it; don't mix in anything trendy or heavy.

**Texture/motifs** — maritime heritage as the visual language: rope, brass, water, the harbor, longleaf pine, the Sandhill crane, sepia archival for "Then & Now." This is what makes it *Gulfport* and not generic-30A.

**The feeling to sell** — Rosemary's sophistication + Seaside's nostalgia + Alys's calm light + Wharf OB's "life happens here" energy for activation. **Editorial, warm, unhurried, specific.**

**Own a signature moment** — Wharf OB has its ferris wheel; Alys has its white arches. Channel South should claim **the rooftop over Jones Park & the Harbor** as its iconic, repeatable, photographable shot. Make it the recurring hero.

---

## WHAT THIS CHANGES FOR THE FEED
1. **Color-grade everything to one coastal-calm look** — instant Alys-level cohesion across renders, photos, graphics. Biggest, fastest visual upgrade.
2. **Lead with architecture + light + water,** lots of white space — restraint, not clutter.
3. **Sell the day, not the drywall** — "life happens here" framing: a morning on the waterfront, sunset on the rooftop (ties to the "hushpitality" trend for Hotel Holden).
4. **Lean the heritage into nostalgia** — the Great Southern Hotel is your Seaside-grade story, and no competitor can copy it.
5. **Keep captions Rosemary-sophisticated** — understated, curated, no hype (already the Marée voice).
6. **Bank a signature rooftop hero shot** and repeat it as the icon.
7. **Hold one main account for now;** revisit Hotel Holden / the Flats sub-accounts near open (the Wharf OB model) — don't fragment a 116-follower base yet.
