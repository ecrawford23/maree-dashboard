## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Germantown Village is a 2024-opened mixed-use retail community center in Gluckstadt, MS—a structural winner in an industry where community centers are the ONLY format gaining foot traffic (+1.22% YoY in 2025-2026) while all competing formats decline. The center's open-air design and community-gathering positioning align with proven format advantages (+10.1% traffic growth in Q1 2024, first to exceed pre-pandemic levels).

**Strategic Opportunity:** Germantown Village's differentiation is NOT a mall with shops—it's a gathering destination that happens to have retail. The social strategy treats it that way.

**July 2026 Objective:** Establish Germantown Village as the go-to gathering space in the Madison County area, drive awareness of tenants as destination (not commodity), and convert social engagement into foot traffic and repeat visits.

---

## I. TARGET AUDIENCE & POSITIONING

### Primary Audience (70% of content focus)
**Local Gluckstadt + Madison County residents, 25–55 years old**
- Own/lease property in the area
- Shop locally; prefer community gathering over isolated mall trips
- Driven by convenience, experience, and social connection (not just transactions)
- Check Instagram/Facebook for events, new tenants, and "what's happening this weekend"
- *Why this segment:* Highest traffic conversion; lowest acquisition cost; highest lifetime value (repeat visits, tenant loyalty)

### Secondary Audience (20% of content focus)
**Greater Jackson area weekend explorers, 20–45 years old**
- Willing to drive 20–30 min for a destination experience (not a mall run)
- Attracted to curated retail + dining + community vibe
- Follow lifestyle/food/community hashtags; influenced by word-of-mouth and social proof
- *Why this segment:* Tourism potential; high engagement if content positions GV as a destination, not a strip mall

### Tertiary Audience (10% of content focus)
**Tenants + tenant employees**
- Want visibility for their brands
- Will tag, share, and generate user-generated content if asked
- Influence friends and family ("I work here" effect)
- *Why this segment:* Organic amplification; ongoing content partners

## II. POSITIONING STATEMENT

**Germantown Village is not a shopping destination. It's a gathering destination that happens to have retail.**

How to talk about it (Marée voice applied):
- Lead with the experience, not the stores
- Name the tenants, but frame them as part of a lifestyle ecosystem
- Talk about what people do at GV (meet friends, discover something new, spend a weekend day) not what they buy
- Show the gathering square, the vibe, the community—retail is the context, not the content
- Be specific: name a tenant, a meal, an experience. Vague language kills credibility ("come shop") while specific language reads as earned positioning ("try the brunch at [X], meet friends in the square, then browse [Y]")

**Example of what NOT to say:**  
"Discover amazing shopping at Germantown Village. We have great stores and restaurants."  
*Why:* Sounds like every mall. Vague. Commodity positioning.

**Example of what TO say:**  
"Saturday morning: coffee at [tenant], farmers market in the square, lunch with friends, browse local boutiques. That's the Germantown Village weekend."  
*Why:* Specific. Experiential. Positions GV as a lifestyle destination, not a shopping mall.

## III. CONTENT PILLARS (July 2026 Focus)

Build the calendar from these 5 pillars. Each post should fit ONE pillar cleanly; multi-pillar posts dilute message.

### 1. TENANT SPOTLIGHTS (30% of posts)
**Goal:** Make tenants the heroes, position retail as part of a lifestyle.

**What to post:**
- Tenant owner/operator story (quick, 1–2 sentences on why they chose GV, what they're building, their ethos)
- Product/service showcase tied to lifestyle (not catalog-style promotions)
- Behind-the-scenes: staff, prep, curation
- Customer moments (with permission) — real people enjoying the tenant's space
- Tenant-hosted events, specials, or launches

**Why it works:**
- Tenants generate UGC when featured (tag, share, invite friends)
- Specific tenant mentions drive foot traffic to that business
- Community sees retail as local operators they know, not faceless chain stores
- Builds tenant relationships (they become ongoing content partners)

**Cadence:** 2–3 posts/week  
**Format:** Instagram Feed (carousel showing operator + space + detail); Reels (owner talking about why GV); Facebook (owner story + customer photo)

**Example post:**
> "Meet [Tenant Name], owners of [Business]. They're obsessed with [specific thing they do]. 'We chose Germantown Village because the community here *actually* gathers. Our customers run into each other, friendships start, it matters.' This Saturday, try their [signature offering]. They're building something real."

---

### 2. GATHERING MOMENTS (25% of posts)
**Goal:** Show the central square and community vibes as the main attraction.

**What to post:**
- Farmers market scenes, crowds in the square, family moments
- Events happening at GV (concerts, festivals, seasonal gatherings)
- Seasonal/weekend vibes (fall setup, summer evening strolls, holiday décor)
- Community member features ("Meet [Name], local, comes every Saturday for...")
- Empty square images with promise ("This Saturday at 9am, the farmers market opens...")

**Why it works:**
- Instagram and Facebook reward lifestyle content over product content
- "Gathering" frames GV as a destination, not a retail location
- Community members see themselves reflected (identity + belonging)
- Drives repeat visits ("I want to be part of that Saturday energy")

**Cadence:** 2–3 posts/week  
**Format:** Instagram Reels (30–60s of gathering clips); Instagram Feed (lifestyle photo + caption on why the moment matters); Facebook (event announcement + community energy)

**Example post:**
> "Saturday mornings. This is why people keep coming back to Germantown Village. The farmers market opens at 9am. Local growers, families catching up, neighbors becoming friends. You in?"

---

### 3. DISCOVER SOMETHING NEW (20% of posts)
**Goal:** Create urgency around new openings, menu items, events, seasonal experiences.

**What to post:**
- New tenant openings (announcement + why it matters for the community)
- Seasonal offerings, limited-time experiences, pop-ups
- New menu items at dining tenants (tied to lifestyle, not just promotion)
- Community improvements or changes to the square
- Collaborations between tenants ("Brunch at [X], browse [Y], coffee from [Z]")

**Why it works:**
- "New" drives engagement and repeat visits
- Creates conversation and word-of-mouth
- Justifies regular follow-up by audience (people check back to see what's next)
- Combats perception that retail centers are static

**Cadence:** 2 posts/week  
**Format:** Instagram Reels (walk-through of new space); Carousel (before/after or multi-detail); Facebook (announcement + event details)

**Example post:**
> "[Tenant Name] opens this Friday. They're bringing [specific thing] to Germantown Village for the first time in the area. We went behind the scenes—watch how they're set up."

---

### 4. LOCAL COMMUNITY VOICE (15% of posts)
**Goal:** Position Germantown Village as embedded in Gluckstadt/Madison County identity.

**What to post:**
- Tenant operators discussing local roots ("Why I moved my business back to Gluckstadt...")
- Seasonal traditions or local events that GV participates in
- Local artist features, collaborations, or installations in the square
- Community feedback/testimonials (quotes from regulars on what GV means to them)
- Tie GV to regional pride, heritage, or values

**Why it works:**
- Builds tribal identity ("This is OUR gathering space")
- Differentiates GV from corporate chains
- Attracts residents who want to support local businesses
- Creates emotional attachment beyond transaction

**Cadence:** 1 post/week  
**Format:** Instagram Stories (testimonial series); Instagram Feed (operator quote + photo); Facebook (longer-form community story)

**Example post:**
> "We asked locals: 'What does Germantown Village mean to you?' Here's what they said. [Testimonial 1] [Testimonial 2] [Testimonial 3] This is why we exist."

---

### 5. PRACTICAL INFO (10% of posts)
**Goal:** Reduce friction, answer frequently asked questions, drive logistics-based traffic.

**What to post:**
- Hours, parking, accessibility info
- Event details and how to register/attend
- Seasonal closures or changes
- Tenant list/map (with visuals, not just text)
- Quick how-tos ("New to farmers market? Here's what to expect...")

**Why it works:**
- Converts interest into action
- Reduces support inquiries
- Improves experience (removes friction)
- Shows operational excellence

**Cadence:** 1 post/week  
**Format:** Instagram Stories (carousel on parking, hours, etc.); Facebook (pinned announcement); Graphics with clear info design

**Example post:**
> "Farmers Market Guide: Hours 9am–1pm Saturdays. Pro tip: Arrive by 10am for best selection. Parking is free. Dog-friendly. Here's what you'll find [carousel of vendor categories]."

## IV. INSTAGRAM VS. FACEBOOK STRATEGY

### INSTAGRAM (Primary Driver of Discovery & Engagement)
**Primary Focus:** Build awareness, drive new visitor conversion, establish GV as a destination

**Content Type Hierarchy:**
1. **Reels** (40% of posts) — Gathering moments, tenant walk-throughs, event snippets, seasonal vibes
   - 30–60 seconds
   - Hook in first 3 seconds (show the vibe, not talking heads)
   - Caption: Specific detail that justifies a visit
2. **Carousel Posts** (35% of posts) — Multi-image storytelling of tenants, events, seasonal experiences
   - 3–5 images per carousel
   - Lead with hero image; each subsequent image adds narrative detail
3. **Feed Photo** (25% of posts) — Single lifestyle/gathering moments that define brand aesthetic
   - High-quality; consistent color palette (warm, community-focused)
   - Caption: One strong sentence + CTA

**Posting Cadence:** 4–5 posts/week (Reels 3x/week, Carousels 1–2x/week, Photos 1x/week)  
**Best Times to Post:** Tuesday–Thursday, 10am or 5–6pm (local time)  
**Hashtag Strategy:** Local (#GluckstadtMS, #MadisonCountyEats, #OpenAirShopping) + Community (#SupportLocal, #SmallBusiness) + Lifestyle (#FarmersMarket, #LocalEats, #GatheringPlaces). Avoid generic retail hashtags.

**CTA on Every Post:** "Save this," "Tag a friend," "Visit Saturday," "Learn more [link in bio]," "See the full list of vendors on our Instagram Stories"

---

### FACEBOOK (Primary Driver of Conversion & Community Building)
**Primary Focus:** Drive attendance, build community dialogue, reach 45+ demographic

**Content Type Hierarchy:**
1. **Event Announcements** (40% of posts) — Farmers market, seasonal events, tenant openings, community gatherings
   - Create Facebook Event (pinned to page)
   - Post includes event details, directions, parking, why it matters
   - Visual: event photo or seasonal hero image
2. **Community Testimonials** (25% of posts) — Customer quotes, tenant owner stories, local voices
   - Photo + quote format
   - Frame as "Meet the people behind GV" or "Why locals love Germantown Village"
3. **Longer-Form Stories** (20% of posts) — Tenant deep-dives, seasonal guides, community features
   - 200–400 words (Facebook users engage with longer captions)
   - Personal tone; conversational voice
4. **Tenant Partnerships/Cross-Promotion** (15% of posts) — Collaborative posts with tenants, joint offers, vendor features
   - Tag tenant page; encourage share-backs
   - Frame as partnership, not one-way promotion

**Posting Cadence:** 3–4 posts/week (Mix of event announcements, testimonials, and stories)  
**Best Times to Post:** Wednesday–Friday, 2–4pm and 6–8pm (local time) — people planning weekend activities  
**Community Engagement:** Reply to comments within 4 hours; respond to messages within 1 business day

**CTA on Every Post:** "Share this event," "Comment with your favorite tenant," "Tag someone who needs to visit," "RSVP on the event page," "Save for Saturday"

## V. VOICE GUARDRAILS (Marée Brand Applied to Germantown Village)

Every post must pass ALL 5 of these Marée-brand voice checks:

### ✅ SPECIFIC (Not Vague)
**Do:** "Try the smoked brisket at [Tenant], browse handmade home goods at [Tenant], catch the live music in the square Saturday at 7pm."  
**Don't:** "We have great food and shopping. Come visit Germantown Village."  
**Why:** Specific language signals you know what you're talking about. Vague language reads as a generic mall.

### ✅ PROOF-LED (Show, Don't Tell)
**Do:** "This farmer has been growing tomatoes for 30 years and brings them to the Germantown Village market every Saturday."  
**Don't:** "We have the best farmers market."  
**Why:** Evidence is more powerful than claims. Let the specifics speak for themselves.

### ✅ WARM + EDITORIAL (Not Corporate)
**Do:** "Sunday morning in the square. Coffee, neighbors chatting, kids running around. This is what we built for."  
**Don't:** "Germantown Village is a premier mixed-use retail destination offering unparalleled shopping and dining experiences."  
**Why:** Sound like a friend inviting you somewhere, not a marketing department.

### ✅ COMMUNITY-FOCUSED (Not Transactional)
**Do:** "Meet Sarah, who owns [Tenant]. She moved back to Gluckstadt to build something here. 'I wanted my business in a place where community actually matters.' Saturday, say hello."  
**Don't:** "[Tenant] is now open. Shop now. Limited-time offer."  
**Why:** GV is about belonging, not shopping. Frame it that way.

### ✅ RESTRAINED (One Strong Idea Per Post)
**Do:** "Farmers market opens at 9am Saturday. Everything you need to know." [Single carousel showing vendors, parking, hours, tips]  
**Don't:** "[Tenant] opens Friday. Also here's all our events, plus our hours, plus a list of all tenants, plus here are 5 reasons to visit, plus save 20% on your first visit, plus..."  
**Why:** One clear message beats multiple muddled ones. Trust the power of a single idea.

### Quality Control Checklist (Before Posting)
For every post, confirm:
- [ ] **Specific?** Did I name a place, tenant, or time?
- [ ] **Proof-led?** Am I showing something, not just claiming it?
- [ ] **Warm?** Does this sound like a friend, not a corporation?
- [ ] **Community-focused?** Is this about gathering/belonging, not transaction?
- [ ] **Restrained?** Is this ONE strong idea, not 5 muddled ones?
- [ ] **CTA clear?** Did I tell people what to do next?
- [ ] **Visuals match voice?** Does the image support the tone?

If you check all 7: Post it.  
If you check 5–6: Edit and try again.  
If you check fewer than 5: Scrap it, start over.
