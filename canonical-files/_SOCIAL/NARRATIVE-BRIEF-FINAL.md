## Four Narrative Pillars (Content Mix)

### Pillar 1: Process (30% of posts)
**What it is:** Behind-the-scenes, naming workshops, discovery sessions, strategy boards, material choices, iteration.

**Why it matters:** Competitors show finished work; nobody shows the *thinking* that got there. This is your whitespace. Showing process builds trust + differentiates.

**Post types:**
- Discovery phase breakdown (what we actually ask clients)
- Naming workshop / wordplay session
- Interior materials as brand language (why brass, why this marble, why this lighting)
- Before/after strategy iteration
- Team working on [project]

**Example captions (Posts 6, 8 template):**
- Short declarative lines
- Sensory specifics (lighting, materials, pathways)
- Developer-intelligent framing (how does this affect behavior, leasing, connection)
- No buzzwords, no hype, no stacked adjectives

---

### Pillar 2: Outcomes (35% of posts)
**What it is:** Finished projects, real estate metrics, proof of impact, case studies with numbers.

**Why it matters:** You can be the only agency talking about leasing velocity, occupancy rate, capital impact. That's your ICP's language. Own it.

**Post types:**
- Gallery Madison / Channel South reveal (with specific details)
- "Here's what we built. Here's what happened to occupancy." (real metrics)
- Project progression (discovery → launch → results)
- Client testimonial (backed by visible results)
- Material detail + outcome (why this choice moved the needle)

**Example captions:**
- "At first glance, this looks like architecture. But the real design is happening between the buildings." (Post 8 template)
- Lead with specificity: name the place, the metric, the outcome
- Close with clear attribution: "// [Project]. @thegallerymadison"

---

### Pillar 3: Philosophy (20% of posts)
**What it is:** Why you approach brand differently, restraint, specificity, the thinking behind the work.

**Why it matters:** Competitors hide behind their work. You can lead with your *thinking*. That's rare and it sells.

**Post types:**
- "The spaces people return to..." (Post 6 template)
- Restraint as luxury (what we leave out matters more)
- "Development is shifting from transactional to experiential"
- "Story is positioning" breakdown
- Brand strategy vs. design vs. interiors (how they're one system)

**Example captions:**
- Short sentences, rhythm, editorial flow
- One strong idea per post
- No vague passion ("We're excited..."). Just declarative truth.

---

### Pillar 4: Team + Behind-the-Scenes (15% of posts)
**What it is:** The people, the studio, the meetings, the collaboration.

**Why it matters:** All competitors hide behind the logo. You're different. Small team. Intentional. Embedded. Make the people visible.

**Post types:**
- Team member intro (Evie, AJ, others) + their approach
- Studio moments (people collaborating, thinking, working)
- Client meeting / workshop vibes
- "A day in the life of..." at Marée
- Team + project outcome (who did what, why they care)

**Example captions:**
- Warm, personal, not corporate
- Why each person matters
- How they think about the work
- Keep it human

## Voice Guardrails (Going Forward)

**Every post MUST:**
1. **Be specific** — Name the place. Name the number. Name the outcome.
2. **Show proof** — What we've actually done, not what we claim.
3. **Use restraint** — One strong idea. Trust white space. No buzzwords.
4. **Sound editorial** — Short sentences. Sensory details. Rhythm.
5. **Be developer-intelligent** — Leasing, occupancy, capital, behavior, connection — not design abstractions.

**Never do:**
- Buzzwords: "best-in-class," "results-driven," "innovative," "leverage," "synergy"
- Stacked adjectives: "beautifully designed, strategically positioned, award-winning"
- Hype: Superlatives, all-caps, fake urgency, "thrilled to announce," "excited to share"
- Vague passion: "We create memorable experiences," "We're passionate about..."
- Emoji after serious statements (🥹✨ undercuts authority)

**Templates that work (from existing posts):**
- Post 6: Short declarative lines + sensory details + developer framing
- Post 7: One-liner + project tag (short, confident, specific)
- Post 8: Observation → details → philosophy → project credit (the full arc)
